About

Dr. Nagahawatta's research focuses on how bioactive molecules — including peptides, polysaccharides, and polyphenolic compounds — modulate cell signaling pathways across diverse disease-relevant contexts such as inflammation, cardiovascular remodeling, sarcopenia, host–pathogen interactions, and metabolic dysfunction. He integrates in silico approaches (molecular docking, structure–activity modeling), in vitro cell-based systems, and in vivo animal models (mouse and zebrafish) to investigate biological mechanisms from target prediction through to functional validation.

His current postdoctoral work at the University of Alberta extends this foundation into cardiovascular biology, where he investigates neprilysin-mediated cardioprotective signaling using global and conditional knockout mouse models, primary cell isolation via Langendorff perfusion, flow cytometry-based immune phenotyping, and biomarker analysis in post-myocardial infarction remodeling.

Prior to joining the University of Alberta, Dr. Nagahawatta served as a postdoctoral researcher at Jeju National University, South Korea, where he led studies on innate immune signaling, antiviral mechanisms targeting SARS-CoV-2, and bioactivity-guided isolation of marine-derived compounds. During this period, he contributed to over 30 publications and mentored 3 PhD-level trainees.

Research Projects

Current

Neprilysin-Mediated Cardioprotective Signaling

Investigating the role of neprilysin across cardiac and immune cell populations using global and cell-specific conditional knockout mouse models to characterize how targeted NEP loss influences post-myocardial infarction remodeling outcomes.

University of Alberta · Department of Medicine
Current

Apelinergic Signaling & Peptide Therapeutics

Evaluating pharmacologic modulation of cardioprotective apelinergic signaling using sacubitril/valsartan and a newly synthesized NEP-resistant apelin-17 analogue as a peptide-based therapeutic strategy.

Published in RSC Medicinal Chemistry, 2026
Completed

Marine Antivirals Against SARS-CoV-2

Multi-target approach using marine polyphenolic compounds to attenuate SARS-CoV-2 cell entry, replication, and cytokine storm — from molecular docking through cell-based antiviral assays and pseudoviral systems.

Ph.D. Thesis · President's Award for Best Research
